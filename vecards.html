Video and Expansion Cards

Video Card (Graphics Card)

  • Description & Function: A video card (GPU) is responsible for rendering images, videos, and animations on a computer screen. It is essential for gaming, video editing, 3D modeling, and AI processing. Some computers use integrated graphics, while others have dedicated graphics cards for higher performance.
  • Troubleshooting Tips:
    • No Display / Black Screen: Check if the card is properly seated in the PCIe slot and if power cables are connected.
    • Driver Issues: Update or reinstall GPU drivers from the manufacturer’s website (NVIDIA, AMD, Intel).
    • Overheating & Performance Issues: Clean dust from fans, ensure good airflow, and monitor temperatures using software like MSI Afterburner.
    • Artifacts (Glitches on Screen): May indicate overheating or failing hardware—try underclocking or replacing the card.
    • Crashes & Freezing: Ensure the power supply is strong enough and check for conflicts with other hardware.

Expansion Card

  • Description & Function: Expansion cards are additional hardware components that plug into a computer’s motherboard to add or enhance functionality. Common types include:
    • Sound Cards: Improve audio quality.
    • Network Cards (NICs): Enable wired or wireless networking.
    • Storage Controller Cards: Add more storage options.
    • Capture Cards: Record or stream video.
  • Troubleshooting Tips:
    • Card Not Detected: Ensure it’s firmly inserted in the slot and check for BIOS/UEFI settings.
    • Driver Problems: Install or update the correct driver for the expansion card.
    • Device Malfunctions: Check for hardware conflicts in Device Manager (Windows) or System Information (Mac).
    • Intermittent Connection: If using a wireless/network card, check antennas and signal strength.
    • Try Another Slot: If the card is still not working, try inserting it into a different PCIe or PCI slot.
